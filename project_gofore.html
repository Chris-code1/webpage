Virtual Meeting Room Redesign

Create the Virtual meeting room of the future! Redesign of the room by applying the Design Sprint 2.0.

The objective of my time at GoFore was to redesign the virtual meeting room. On arrival the initial design was a table and chairs on which all the participants were seated. After applying the Design Spring 2.0 the meeting Room looked the following.

So for the workshop organised we followed the Design Sprint 2.0 by Jake Knapp, which is the evolved methodology of the initial The Sprint book. The Sprint has as an objective to define the problem, create solutions, build a prototype and finally test this prototype within only 4 days in our team of 5 people.

During the first day objectives and targets were chosen, people were invited for interviews and the problem was mapped down.

On the second day different ideas and solutions were developped and sketched out by all the members.

The third day a decision was taken which idea to prototype out of the 5 amazing sketches created by each of the members. Some hidden art talent was revealed here ;)!

After having in mind what we wanted to test and which questions we wanted to answer, it was finally time to get our hands dirty and create a prototype. Challenging here was to prototype in VR. With normal 2D interfaces there are quite some tricks on how to create high fidelity prototypes within one day. For VR this was something completely new to us. But following you see the outcome :)

The day we all waited for! Testing day! Earning the fruits of our work intense week. For the testing we had two rooms. One room in which I introduced and guided the test person through the user test scenario. In another room next door the whole team was sitting in front of a big screen on which we set up a video call so they could have a good view of what was going on. They all were armed with post its and pens to not miss out on any detail they observed. After 4 tests, we gathered together and discussed the insights we got from the session.
