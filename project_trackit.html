Track It

Data aggregator and activity tracking from programmed stop preparation to its follow-up! To view the conference poster click here! for the publication here!

During my time at CERN I accompanied the project from initial ideation up until release of version 1.1 and handover to the users. We applied the Human Centered Design Process in order to develop a software tool at CERN within the Enginering Department that would help during the preparation, implementation and follow up of projects within the accelerator complex.

An agile innovation cycle was applied in order to iteratively improve the product.

First a large number of interviews and observations were done in order to better understand the problem, the current way of working and the context.

Once the problem was understood, first low-fidelity paper prototype was produced using InkScape. The low-fidelity prototyped helped then to better communicate the collected needs and how we thought about targeting them. Also could we perform first User tests and collect feedback which led to several for and backs of improving the lo-fi prototype.

After agreeing on a good concept, the prototype went into production and a first high fidelity version was created by our development team.

Through having a first interactive high fidelity prototype further feedback and testing could be done. With the information collected the project iteratively proceeded to version 1 and 1.1.
